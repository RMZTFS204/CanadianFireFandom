Fire Safety

Simple, practical fire safety tips commonly shared across Canada.

Emergency Number

To report a fire or emergency, dial 9-1-1 immediately from a safe location.

If a Fire Happens

  • Get out, stay out, and call 9-1-1 from a safe location.
  • If there is smoke, stay low and crawl to the nearest exit.
  • If your clothes catch fire: Stop, Drop, and Roll.
  • Do not go back inside a burning building for any reason.
  • Meet at a pre-determined safe meeting place outside your home.

Smoke Alarms

  • Install smoke alarms on every level of your home, inside bedrooms, and outside sleeping areas.
  • Test smoke alarms monthly and replace batteries at least once a year.
  • Replace smoke alarms every 10 years or as recommended by the manufacturer.

Escape Plan

  • Create a home escape plan with at least two ways out of every room.
  • Practice your escape plan at least twice a year with everyone in your household.
  • Ensure windows and doors can be opened easily and are not blocked.

Kitchen Safety

  • Never leave cooking unattended on the stove or in the oven.
  • Keep flammable items away from heat sources.
  • If a grease fire starts, do not use water—smother the flames with a lid or fire extinguisher.

Electrical Safety

  • Do not overload electrical outlets or power strips.
  • Replace damaged cords or frayed wires immediately.
  • Turn off electrical appliances when not in use.

Heating Safety

  • Keep anything flammable at least one metre away from heaters or fireplaces.
  • Have heating equipment inspected regularly by a certified professional.
  • Use fireplace screens to prevent sparks from escaping.

Outdoor Fire Safety

  • Only use fire pits or campfires in approved areas.
  • Never leave fires unattended, and fully extinguish them before leaving.
  • Check local fire bans and restrictions before burning outdoors.

Fire Extinguishers

  • Keep fire extinguishers in accessible locations in your home.
  • Know how to use a fire extinguisher: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep (PASS).
  • Replace or service extinguishers according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

General Tips

  • Teach children the emergency number and fire safety rules.
  • Keep flammable liquids stored safely and away from heat sources.
  • Regularly check and maintain all fire safety equipment.
  • Stay calm, think ahead, and always have a plan.
